Did you know?

Blood makes up about 7% of your body's weight.

The average adult has about 1.2 to 1.5 gallons of blood.

Blood carries oxygen and nutrients to your body's cells and removes waste products.

Blood is composed of plasma, red blood cells, white blood cells, and platelets.

Red blood cells contain hemoglobin, which is a protein that carries oxygen from the lungs to the body's tissues.

White blood cells are part of the immune system and help defend the body against infection.

Platelets help stop bleeding by forming blood clots.

Blood type is determined by the presence or absence of certain proteins on the surface of red blood cells.

Blood is constantly being produced and replenished in the body. On average, the human body produces about 2.5 million red blood cells every second.

Blood can be donated and transfused to help save the lives of people who have lost blood due to injury or illness.

The average adult has about 5 liters of blood in their body.

Blood is important for regulating body temperature and maintaining a healthy pH balance in the body.
